Research Philosophy

I got into this trying to understand how Web3 could work for communities that traditional finance ignores. Turns out the answer involves a lot of "first understand how it breaks."

The stablecoin research made it clear: you can't have perfect decentralization AND perfect stability AND perfect capital efficiency at the same time. Pick two. The attack modeling showed me that most vulnerabilities live between systems—oracle-circuit breaker interactions that look fine individually but create exploits when combined.

I can model sophisticated economic attacks and then explain them to central bank officials in the same week. I can design regenerative finance products and stress-test them with adversarial scenarios. Most people do one or the other.

What I bring: PhD-level methodology, experience validating products with Brazilian regulators, and enough time in emerging markets to know which Web3 ideas are bullshit and which might actually work.

Currently building the "DeFi Infrastructures for Regeneration Market" series—figuring out how AMMs, lending protocols, and stablecoins can support carbon credits and biodiversity markets without getting exploited.
