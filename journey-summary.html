Research Philosophy

I solve problems by understanding systems deeply, then finding where they break.

My research evolved from "how does this work?" to "how could someone exploit this?" The stablecoin analysis taught me that the most interesting insights come from examining trade-offs—you can't have perfect decentralization AND perfect stability AND perfect capital efficiency. The attack modeling taught me that security vulnerabilities often hide in the gaps between systems—like oracle-circuit breaker interactions that look safe individually but create exploitable patterns when combined.

What makes my approach different: I can model sophisticated attacks, then explain them to central bank officials. I can design regenerative finance products, then stress-test them with adversarial scenarios. I move between technical depth and institutional communication without losing rigor in either direction.

What I bring to security research: Academic methodology + real-world validation experience + emerging markets context. I don't just find vulnerabilities—I translate them into actionable insights for different stakeholders.

Current focus: Building the "DeFi Infrastructures for Regeneration Market" series, exploring how AMMs, lending protocols, and stablecoins can support carbon credits, biodiversity markets, and regenerative agriculture while maintaining security against economic attacks.
