Research Evolution

From Early ReFi Design to Regulatory Validation (2021-2025)

My Web3 research journey began with the initial design of ReFi Spring, focusing on audience projection, grant allocation systems, and allocation tracking. Meanwhile, I accumulated articles and research on the intersection of blockchain and international development at Impact Plus.

Returning to Brazil, I founded ReFaz0x, served as a judge at the hackathon organized by SERPRO in partnership with Brazil's Ministry of Economy to tokenize all federal real estate assets, and contributed to the Blockteam which won awards in two of the five tracks of the Brazilian National Treasury debt tokenization challenge.

Through ReFaz0x, we started participating in Ekonavi where, as head of research, I led the tokenomics design and the product presented in the LIFT Labs program. Ekonavi validated its thesis for impact asset modeling in Brazil, becoming one of the finalists of the program that also gave rise to PIX, Brazil's largest payment system.

My latest research adventure was an analysis of stablecoins USDC, DAI, and Bread, along with experimenting with stablecoin designs for RWA, and finally, modeling attacks on DeFi protocols.
